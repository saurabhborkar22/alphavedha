2.2 Preprocessing Pipeline

Every row of data passes through 5 sequential processing stages before features are computed. Order matters.

Corporate Action Adjustment
Adjusts all historical prices for stock splits, bonus issues, and rights issues. A 1:5 split means all prices before the split date are divided by 5. Without this, a stock that splits from Rs.5000 to Rs.1000 looks like an 80% crash. Every price column (open, high, low, close) and volume are adjusted. An audit log tracks every adjustment made.
Circuit Limit Detection
Indian stocks have circuit breakers at 5%, 10%, and 20%. When a stock hits its circuit, trading halts and the price is artificially frozen. These days produce misleading signals. This step flags circuit-hit days with a boolean column so downstream features can handle them appropriately (usually by excluding from volatility and momentum calculations).
Missing Data Handling
Stocks can be suspended for days or weeks. Forward-fill is used with a maximum gap of 10 days. An is_suspended flag marks filled rows. Beyond 10 days, the gap is left as-is to avoid propagating stale data. This is critical for backtesting — treating suspended stocks as tradeable would be a dangerous error.
Fractional Differentiation (FFD)
Raw price series are non-stationary (they trend upward over time). Standard differencing (returns) makes them stationary but destroys memory. Fractional differentiation finds the minimum d (between 0.1 and 0.8) that makes the series stationary (ADF p-value < 0.05) while preserving maximum memory. The optimal d is calibrated per stock using the ADF test and recomputed monthly.
Outlier Winsorization
Extreme values are capped at the 1st and 99th percentiles. This prevents rare events (flash crashes, fat-finger trades) from dominating model training. Values below the 1st percentile are set to the 1st percentile value; above the 99th are set to the 99th. Applied per feature, per stock.

03 Feature Engineering

159 features across 9 categories computed from raw data

Critical Rule: No Look-Ahead Bias Every feature is computed using ONLY past data available at prediction time. All rolling windows look backward. No future data leaks into any feature. This is the most common mistake in quant finance ML — AlphaVedha enforces it at every level.
Technical Indicators
Classical price and volume-based signals
  • Trend: SMA (10/20/50/200d), EMA (12/26d), MACD + signal
  • Momentum: RSI (14d), Stochastic %K/%D, Williams %R, CCI
  • Volatility: Bollinger Bands (width + %B), ATR (14d), NATR
  • Trend Strength: ADX (14d), +DI, -DI
  • Volume: OBV, Volume SMA ratio, VWAP distance
Macro Features
Market-wide and economy-level indicators
  • Volatility: India VIX level, VIX change, VIX percentile
  • Institutional: FII net flow (5d/10d), DII net flow, FII-DII divergence
  • Indices: Nifty 50 return, Bank Nifty return, sector index moves
  • Currency: USD/INR rate, INR momentum
  • Commodities: Crude oil price, oil change %, gold movement
  • Global: US overnight return (S&P 500 proxy)
  • Economy: GST collections, PMI, credit growth, forex reserves
Derivatives Features
Forward-looking signals from India's massive F&O market
  • Open Interest: Futures OI change %, buildup (long/short/covering/unwinding)
  • Options: Put-Call Ratio (OI + volume), max pain distance
  • Volatility: IV percentile (vs 252d), IV skew
  • Greeks: Aggregate delta, gamma, vega exposure
  • Basis: Futures premium/discount vs spot
Fundamental Features
Financial health and valuation metrics
  • Valuation: P/E ratio, P/B ratio, dividend yield
  • Profitability: ROE, operating margin, net margin
  • Leverage: Debt/Equity ratio
  • Growth: EPS growth, revenue growth QoQ
  • Earnings: Surprise %, days since earnings, beat streak
Microstructure Features
India-unique trading mechanics
  • Delivery: Delivery % (India-unique: % of traded volume that becomes actual holdings), delivery Z-score, delivery trend
  • Breakout: High delivery + price breakout signal (delivery > 60% AND close > 20d high)
  • Liquidity: Average daily turnover, bid-ask spread estimate
  • Order Flow: Volume imbalance, VWAP deviation
Sentiment Features
Natural language signals from financial news
  • Score: FinBERT sentiment (bearish/neutral/bullish) per stock
  • Trend: 1-day and 5-day rolling sentiment average
  • Momentum: Sentiment improving or deteriorating?
  • Volume: News volume Z-score (event detection)
  • Sector: Average sentiment across sector peers
Calendar Features
Time-based patterns unique to Indian markets
  • Day patterns: Day of week, month, quarter (markets show weekday effects)
  • Events: Days to/from earnings, F&O expiry (monthly/weekly)
  • Rebalancing: Days to Nifty 50 rebalancing (March/September)
  • Holidays: NSE trading holiday calendar
Returns Features
Statistical properties of return distributions
  • Returns: Log returns (1d, 5d, 10d, 20d, 60d)
  • Volatility: Rolling volatility (10d, 20d, 60d)
  • Risk-adjusted: Rolling Sharpe ratio, Sortino ratio
  • Distribution: Skewness, kurtosis (fat tails = higher risk)
Alternative Data Features
Non-traditional leading indicators
  • Auto sales: Monthly volumes for Maruti, Tata Motors, M&M
  • Cement: Monthly dispatch for UltraTech, Shree, ACC
  • Digital: UPI transaction volume (proxy for banking)
  • Energy: Power consumption, crude oil daily
  • Trade: Port cargo volume, forex reserves weekly
  • Economy: GST collections, PMI, steel production

04 ML Models

5 independent base models, each capturing different patterns

Why Multiple Models? No single model can capture all market patterns. XGBoost excels at tabular feature interactions, LSTM captures temporal dependencies, TFT adds attention for long-range patterns, GNN models inter-stock relationships, and HMM detects regime changes. Their disagreement itself is a signal.
XGBoost
Gradient Boosted Trees
  • Strength: Tabular data king
  • Input: 159 features (flat)
  • Output: Direction (3-class) + magnitude
  • Training: Dual models (classifier + regressor)
  • Serialization: joblib
  • Feature importance: Built-in (gain-based)
  • Speed: Fastest inference
LSTM
Recurrent Neural Network
  • Strength: Sequential patterns
  • Input: 30-day sequences
  • Architecture: 2-layer, 128 hidden units
  • Output: Direction probs + magnitude
  • Training: PyTorch, GPU-accelerated
  • Serialization: safetensors
  • Early stopping: Val loss patience
Temporal Fusion Transformer
Attention-Based Network
  • Strength: Long-range dependencies
  • Architecture: GRN + VSN + Multi-Head Attention
  • Multi-horizon: 7d, 15d, 30d predictions
  • Interpretable: Attention weights show which time steps matter
  • Variable selection: Auto-learns feature importance
  • Serialization: safetensors
GraphSAGE (GNN)
Graph Neural Network
  • Strength: Inter-stock relationships
  • Architecture: 2-layer GraphSAGE (pure PyTorch)
  • Graph: Sector + correlation + promoter edges
  • Key insight: When TCS reports, Infosys/Wipro should update
  • Aggregation: Mean neighbor features
  • No dependency: No torch_geometric needed
HMM Regime Detector
Hidden Markov Model
  • Purpose: Market state classification
  • States: Bull, Bear, Sideways, High-Volatility
  • Library: hmmlearn (Gaussian HMM)
  • Output: State probabilities [4-dim vector]
  • Usage: Adjusts strategy per regime
  • Auto-labels: States labeled by mean return + vol

4.1 Training Discipline

What We Do (Correct)

  • Temporal train/val splits (never random for time-series)
  • 20-day embargo between train and val sets
  • Purging: remove overlapping labels at split boundary
  • CPCV: Combinatorial Purged Cross-Validation for robust model selection
  • Out-of-fold (OOF) predictions for ensemble training
  • Fractionally differentiated features (not raw prices)
  • Sample weights with decay (recent data matters more)
  • Early stopping on validation loss
  • Gradient clipping (max_norm=1.0) for stability

What We Avoid (Common Mistakes)

  • Random train/test splits (future data leaks into training)
  • Training on raw prices (non-stationary = garbage model)
  • Using the same data for training and validation
  • Ignoring survivorship bias (only using stocks that still exist)
  • Not adjusting for corporate actions before computing features
  • Including circuit-hit days in momentum calculations
  • Optimizing on in-sample metrics (overfitting)
  • Ignoring transaction costs in backtest

4.2 Labeling: Triple Barrier Method

Instead of predicting "will the stock go up tomorrow?", we use the Triple Barrier method from Marcos Lopez de Prado. For each day, three barriers are set:

BarrierTriggerLabel
Upper Barrier (Take Profit)Price rises by 2.0x ATR+1 (Buy)
Lower Barrier (Stop Loss)Price falls by 1.5x ATR-1 (Sell)
Time Barrier (Holding Period)Neither barrier hit within max days0 (Hold)

The asymmetric barriers (2.0x up vs 1.5x down) create a natural "let profits run, cut losses short" bias. ATR (Average True Range) makes barriers adaptive to each stock's volatility.

06 Prediction Engine

The full 10-step pipeline from features to final prediction

Receive Request
Input: symbol (e.g., "TCS"), feature DataFrame (159 columns), current price, market-level features (VIX, FII flows, index returns). Can be a single stock or batch of up to 20.
Regime Detection (HMM)
Runs HMM on market-level features to classify the current regime. Returns 4 probabilities: [P(bull), P(bear), P(sideways), P(high_vol)]. If market features unavailable, defaults to uniform [0.25, 0.25, 0.25, 0.25]. The detected regime drives strategy selection.
Base Model Predictions
Runs all 3 base models (XGBoost, LSTM, TFT) independently. Each returns: direction probabilities [3], magnitude estimate, confidence score. Graceful degradation: if one model fails, it gets neutral predictions [0.33, 0.34, 0.33]. Minimum 2 of 3 must succeed, otherwise the entire prediction fails with PredictionError.
Stacking Ensemble
Combines 14 meta-features (3 models x 3 probs + 4 regime probs + 1 disagreement) through the RidgeClassifier meta-learner. Produces: calibrated direction (-1, 0, +1), calibrated confidence (0-1), ensemble probability distribution.
Meta-Labeling Gate
Binary XGBClassifier evaluates: "given these features AND the ensemble prediction, is this trade likely to be correct?" If P(correct) >= 0.55, the signal is marked is_tradeable = True. Below 0.55, the signal is still returned but flagged as non-tradeable — useful for monitoring.
Conformal Prediction
Computes 90% prediction intervals using MAPIE jackknife+. Converts magnitude estimate into three price targets: low, mid, high. Wide intervals indicate high uncertainty — a useful risk signal even beyond the direction prediction.
Composite Scoring
Computes a 0-100 composite score from 6 weighted sub-scores: technical momentum (25%), derivatives sentiment (20%), macro alignment (20%), microstructure quality (15%), news sentiment (10%), volatility risk (10%). Missing feature groups have weights redistributed to available groups.
Regime Strategy Selection
Based on the detected regime, adjusts thresholds and strategy parameters:
Risk Assessment
Position sizing via Half-Kelly formula, portfolio constraint checks (sector cap, correlation, liquidity), and circuit breaker evaluation. See Section 07 for full details.
Output: StockPrediction
Final output contains 16 fields: symbol, timestamp, direction, magnitude, composite_score, meta_confidence, is_tradeable, regime, regime_probabilities, price_target_low/mid/high, model_disagreement, position_size_pct, model_version, and any warnings from degraded models.

07 Risk Management

Every prediction passes through 4 layers of risk control

Risk Management is Non-Negotiable No prediction reaches the output without passing through position sizing, portfolio constraints, and circuit breaker evaluation. This layer exists to protect capital from model errors, regime changes, and black swan events.

7.1 Position Sizing: Half-Kelly Criterion

The Kelly Criterion calculates the mathematically optimal bet size to maximize long-term wealth growth. We use Half-Kelly because full Kelly is too aggressive for noisy markets. 

kelly_fraction = (2 * meta_confidence - 1)    # e.g., 0.65 confidence -> 0.30
half_kelly = kelly_fraction * 0.5 * 100        # -> 15% of portfolio
position_size = min(half_kelly, max_single_stock_pct)  # capped at 10%

# Guards:
# - meta_confidence < min_threshold (0.55) -> size = 0%
# - magnitude <= 0 -> size = 0%
# - negative kelly -> size = 0%

7.3 Circuit Breaker (Drawdown Protection)

Modeled after NSE's market-wide circuit breakers but applied to the portfolio. Three escalation levels with automatic position reduction.

Level 1: Warning
Trigger: Portfolio drawdown reaches 10%
Action: Halve all position sizes (50% reduction)
Effect: New and existing positions both affected
Level 2: Alert
Trigger: Portfolio drawdown reaches 15%
Action: Block all new entries; existing positions at 50%
Effect: Only exits allowed, no new risk
Level 3: Emergency
Trigger: Portfolio drawdown reaches 20%
Action: Close all positions (size = 0%)
Effect: Full de-risk until recovery

Recovery: When portfolio value recovers to 95% of peak value, circuit breaker resets to Level 0 and normal trading resumes.

08 Execution Engine

Almgren-Chriss market impact model calibrated for Indian markets

The execution engine determines WHEN and HOW to place orders to minimize slippage — the difference between the predicted price and the actual execution price.

8.1 Market Impact Model

Based on the Almgren-Chriss framework, adapted for Indian market microstructure:

# Total impact = temporary impact + permanent impact

temporary_impact = eta * sigma * (order_size / avg_daily_volume) ^ 0.6
permanent_impact = gamma * sigma * (order_size / avg_daily_volume)
bid_ask_cost = spread based on cap tier

# Cap-tier calibrated spreads:
#   Large cap (Nifty 50):  5 basis points  (0.05%)
#   Mid cap (Midcap 150):  10 basis points (0.10%)
#   Small cap:             20 basis points (0.20%)

total_impact = temporary_impact + permanent_impact + bid_ask_cost

09 Graph Neural Network

Modeling inter-stock relationships with GraphSAGE

Stocks don't move in isolation. When TCS reports earnings, Infosys, Wipro, and HCL Tech should update their expectations. When the Tata Group makes a strategic move, all Tata companies are affected. The GNN captures these relationships.

9.1 Stock Graph Construction

The graph has 50+ nodes (stocks) connected by 3 types of edges:

Sector Edges
Stocks in the same sector are connected. E.g., TCS-Infosys-Wipro-HCL (IT sector), HDFC-ICICI-SBI-Kotak (Banking). Loaded from configs/stocks.yaml. Weight: 1.0.
Correlation Edges
Computed dynamically from rolling return correlations. Stocks with correlation >= 0.6 get connected. Weight: absolute correlation value. Cross-sector pairs like Reliance-ONGC emerge naturally.
Promoter Group Edges
Stocks owned by the same business group. E.g., Tata Motors-TCS-Titan-Tata Steel (Tata Group), Reliance-Jio (Ambani). From configs/stocks.yaml. Weight: 1.0.

9.2 GraphSAGE Architecture

# Pure PyTorch implementation (no torch_geometric dependency)

Layer 1: GraphSAGELayer(159 features -> 64 hidden)
  - For each node: aggregate neighbor features (mean)
  - Concatenate: [self_features || neighbor_aggregate] (128 dim)
  - Linear transform: 128 -> 64
  - ReLU activation
  - Dropout 0.3

Layer 2: GraphSAGELayer(64 -> 64)
  - Same aggregation on 64-dim embeddings
  - Second hop of neighborhood

Classification Head: Linear(64 -> 3)  # Sell / Hold / Buy
Regression Head:     Linear(64 -> 1)  # Magnitude

Training: Full-batch (all nodes at once, not mini-batch)
Loss: 0.7 * CrossEntropy + 0.3 * MSE (weighted by sample weights)
Key Insight Without graph structure (empty edges), GraphSAGE degrades to a standard MLP — the neighbor aggregation produces zeros, and only self-features drive predictions. This means the GNN model gracefully handles stocks with no relationships.

10 Online Learning & Monitoring

Detecting model drift and triggering retraining automatically

Markets evolve. A model trained in a bull market will fail in a bear market. Online learning monitors model health in real-time and triggers retraining before accuracy degrades.

10.1 Drift Detection

Two statistical tests run on every feature, comparing recent data against training data:

PSI (Population Stability Index)

Measures how much a feature's distribution has shifted from training time. Divides values into 10 quantile bins and compares bin frequencies. 

PSI = sum((current% - reference%) * ln(current% / reference%))

PSI < 0.1  -> No drift (stable)
PSI 0.1-0.2 -> Warning (moderate shift)
PSI > 0.2  -> Alert (significant drift, retrain!)

KS Test (Kolmogorov-Smirnov)

Non-parametric test comparing two distributions. Returns a statistic (0-1) and p-value. Low p-value = distributions are different = feature has drifted. 

statistic, p_value = ks_2samp(reference, current)

# Used as secondary confirmation alongside PSI
# If both PSI > 0.2 AND KS p-value < 0.05
#   -> strong evidence of drift

10.3 Retraining Triggers

Three independent triggers can fire retraining. Any one is sufficient:

Calendar Trigger
Frequency: Weekly (configurable)
Logic: If days since last training >= schedule interval
Why: Ensures models stay fresh even without explicit degradation
Drift Trigger
Condition: Any feature PSI > 0.2
Logic: DriftReport.requires_retrain = True
Why: Feature distributions have shifted; model was trained on different data
Performance Trigger
Condition: Rolling accuracy < min_accuracy (48%)
Logic: PerformanceReport.requires_retrain = True
Why: Model is underperforming; market dynamics have changed

10.4 Shadow Deployment

Retrain Triggered
New model trained on rolling 2-year window using latest data. CPCV validation must pass.
Shadow Registration
New model registered as "shadow" status. It generates predictions in parallel with the active model but predictions are logged, not acted upon.
Shadow Period (20 Trading Days)
Both models run for 20 trading days (~1 month). Shadow model's predictions are compared against actual outcomes to verify performance.
Promotion Decision
If shadow model outperforms or matches the active model, it's promoted to "active" status. The previous active model is retired. If shadow underperforms, it's discarded. Last 5 versions are kept for rollback.

What: Predictions are already generated and stored. Paper trading module logs the "entry prices" at market open. If using real trading (future), execution engine determines optimal entry timing (prefer 10:30-11:30 window, not opening auction).
Action: No cron needed — this is real-time or manual
3:45 PM IST — Outcome Evaluation
What: After market close, compare today's predictions against actual outcomes. Update paper trading P&L. Store prediction-vs-actual pairs for monitoring.
How: cron: 45 15 * * 1-5
Updates: Rolling accuracy (7d/30d/90d), profitable %, alpha vs Nifty 50
4:00 PM IST — Drift Check
What: Run drift detector on today's features vs training distribution. Check PSI and KS test for each feature. Run performance monitor on recent predictions. If retrain is triggered, queue it for overnight.
How: cron: 0 16 * * 5 (weekly, Fridays only)
Duration: ~30 seconds
11:00 PM IST — Retraining (If Triggered)
What: If drift/performance/calendar trigger fired, retrain on rolling 2-year window. Run CPCV validation. If passes, deploy as shadow model.
How: cron: 0 23 * * 6 (Saturday night, if trigger set)
Command: alphavedha train all
Duration: ~20-30 minutes for all models

14.3 CPCV (Combinatorial Purged Cross-Validation)

Standard k-fold CV is invalid for time-series (data leakage). CPCV generates all combinatorial train/test splits while purging overlapping labels at boundaries and applying embargo (20-day gap). This produces a distribution of model performance, not a single overfit number.

16 Glossary

Key terms and concepts used throughout this system

TermDefinition
ATRAverage True Range — volatility measure based on daily high-low range over 14 days
CPCVCombinatorial Purged Cross-Validation — time-series-safe model evaluation method
Conformal PredictionStatistical method to produce prediction intervals with guaranteed coverage probability
Delivery %India-unique metric: percentage of traded shares actually delivered (transferred to demat). High delivery = genuine buying, not just day-trading
DrawdownPeak-to-trough decline in portfolio value. Max drawdown is the worst such decline ever.
EmbargoGap (20 days) between training and validation data to prevent label leakage at boundaries
FFDFractionally Differentiated Series — makes price series stationary while preserving memory
FIIForeign Institutional Investors — foreign funds investing in Indian equities
DIIDomestic Institutional Investors — Indian mutual funds, insurance companies, pension funds
F&OFutures & Options — derivatives segment of NSE. India has the world's largest options market.
GNNGraph Neural Network — neural network that operates on graph structures (nodes + edges)
GraphSAGEGraph Sample and Aggregate — GNN variant that learns by aggregating neighbor features
Half-KellyHalf of the Kelly Criterion bet size — optimal growth rate with reduced volatility
HMMHidden Markov Model — probabilistic model for detecting hidden market regimes
LSTMLong Short-Term Memory — RNN variant that captures temporal dependencies in sequences
MAPIEModel Agnostic Prediction Interval Estimator — library for conformal prediction
Meta-LabelingBinary classifier that predicts whether the primary model's prediction is correct. Quality gate.
OIOpen Interest — total number of outstanding derivative contracts. Changes indicate new money flow.
OOFOut-of-Fold — predictions made on data the model didn't train on (used for ensemble training)
PCRPut-Call Ratio — ratio of put options to call options. High PCR = bearish sentiment.
PEADPost-Earnings Announcement Drift — stocks that beat estimates continue outperforming 60-90 days
PSIPopulation Stability Index — measures distribution shift between two datasets (drift detection)
Profit FactorGross profits divided by gross losses. > 1.0 means profitable, > 1.8 is excellent.
PurgingRemoving training samples whose labels overlap with validation data at the boundary
RegimeMarket state — bull (trending up), bear (trending down), sideways, or high-volatility
Sharpe RatioRisk-adjusted return = (return - risk-free rate) / volatility. > 1.5 is excellent.
SlippageDifference between expected execution price and actual price. Caused by market impact.
STTSecurities Transaction Tax — Indian tax on stock transactions (0.1% delivery, 0.025% intraday)
TFTTemporal Fusion Transformer — attention-based model for multi-horizon time-series prediction
Triple BarrierLabeling method: profit target (up), stop loss (down), time limit — first barrier hit determines label
VWAPVolume Weighted Average Price — fair price based on volume distribution throughout the day
Walk-ForwardBacktest method: train on past, predict next period, roll forward. Simulates real-world usage.
WinsorizationCapping extreme values at percentiles (e.g., 1st and 99th) to reduce outlier influence
